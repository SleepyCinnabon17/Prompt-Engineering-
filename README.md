## 📚 **About This Repository**

This project explores how **LLMs behave under different prompting strategies**, combining:

- 🪞 **Mirror Journal (Part A)** → Challenging a real personal belief  
- ⚙️ **Technical Practicum (Part B)** → Structured prompt experimentation  

> ⚠️ LLMs are not oracles. They are systems that require control, skepticism, and evaluation.

---

## 🪞 **Mirror Journal**

### 🧠 Belief Explored
> *“All religions are misogynistic at their core…”*
Something bold, something sensitive...something worth debating.

---

### 🔍 **Mirror Sessions Breakdown**

| Session | Technique | Outcome |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Zero-shot | Broad, diplomatic → missed specificity |
| 2 | Role Prompting | Skeptic vs Defender → converged to power structures |
| 3 | Few-shot | More nuance, but echoed bias |
| 4 | Chain-of-Thought | Exposed reasoning flaws |
| 5 | Self-Consistency | Stability ≠ truth |
| 6 | Metacognitive | Found uncertainty, missed blind spots |
| 7 | Synthesis | Belief refined, not replaced |

---

### ⚠️ **Key Insight**

> The AI doesn’t argue with you.  
> It negotiates with your prompt.

Even when challenged, it tends to:
- Default to safe, balanced conclusions  
- Avoid strong commitments  
- Mirror your framing unless tightly controlled

## 🔬 **Experiments Conducted**

### 🧠 Chain-of-Thought vs Direct Prompting
- Improves reasoning visibility  
- Still produces **confident but flawed logic**

---

### 🔁 Self-Consistency
- Same prompt → different outputs  
- Majority voting helps, but:

> Consistency ≠ correctness

---

### 📚 RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
- Improves factual grounding  
- But:

> Garbage context = confident garbage output

---

### ⚠️ LLM Limitations (Tested)

- ❌ Hallucination → fabricated claims  
- ⚖️ Bias → directional framing  
- 🧠 Context Loss → ignores key info mid-prompt
